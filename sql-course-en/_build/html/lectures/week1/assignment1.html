Assignment 1¶

Entity Relationship Model¶

Question 1:¶

Make an entity relationship model (E-R):

1. We have a university in which there are several courses and special workshops. Each workshop is directed by a teacher who can direct several workshops. Special workshops demand many resources, so that only allows that each student enrolls to one workshop of this type. On the other hand, a course consists of several subjects. Each of them has a number of credits. Students can enroll to the subjects they want. Finally, the student receives a grade for each subject, at the end of the course.

2. We want to make a database with discs that we have at home. A disc can have a singer or a group, or more. Also a disc has a record. We are going to complicate the example the previous example: now we must keep in mind that a disc is composed of songs. These can be written by the same person who sings, but often they are different people.

Multiple-choice questions¶

Question 1:¶

Suppose relation R(A,B,C) has the following tuples

\[\begin{split}\begin{array}{|c|c|c|} \hline \textbf{A} & \textbf{B} & \textbf{C} \\ \hline 1 & 2 & 3 \\ \hline 4 & 2 & 3 \\ \hline 4 & 5 & 6 \\ \hline 2 & 5 & 3 \\ \hline 1 & 2 & 6 \\ \hline \end{array}\end{split}\]

and relation S(A,B,C) has the following tuples:

\[\begin{split}\begin{array}{|c|c|c|} \hline \textbf{A} & \textbf{B} & \textbf{C} \\ \hline 2 & 5 & 3 \\ \hline 2 & 5 & 4 \\ \hline 4 & 5 & 6 \\ \hline 1 & 2 & 3 \\ \hline \end{array}\end{split}\]

Compute the intersection of the relations R and S. Which of the following tuples is in the result?

  1. (4,5,6)
  2. (1,2,6)
  3. (4,2,3)
  4. (2,4,3)

Question 2:¶

Suppose relation R(A,B,C) has the following tuples:

\[\begin{split}\begin{array}{|c|c|c|} \hline \textbf{A} & \textbf{B} & \textbf{C} \\ \hline 1 & 2 & 3 \\ \hline 4 & 2 & 3 \\ \hline 4 & 5 & 6 \\ \hline 2 & 5 & 3 \\ \hline 1 & 2 & 6 \\ \hline \end{array}\end{split}\]

and relation S(A,B,C) has the following tuples:

\[\begin{split}\begin{array}{|c|c|c|} \hline \textbf{A} & \textbf{B} & \textbf{C} \\ \hline 2 & 5 & 3 \\ \hline 2 & 5 & 4 \\ \hline 4 & 5 & 6 \\ \hline 1 & 2 & 3 \\ \hline \end{array}\end{split}\]

Compute (R - S) union (S - R) often called the “symmetric difference” of R and S. Which of the following tuples is in the result?

  1. (2,2,3)
  2. (4,2,3)
  3. (4,5,6)
  4. (4,5,3)

Question 3:¶

Suppose relation R(A,B,C) has the following tuples:

\[\begin{split}\begin{array}{|c|c|c|} \hline \textbf{A} & \textbf{B} & \textbf{C} \\ \hline 1 & 2 & 3 \\ \hline 4 & 2 & 3 \\ \hline 4 & 5 & 6 \\ \hline 2 & 5 & 3 \\ \hline 1 & 2 & 6 \\ \hline \end{array}\end{split}\]

and relation S(A,B,C) has de following tuples:

\[\begin{split}\begin{array}{|c|c|c|} \hline \textbf{A} & \textbf{B} & \textbf{C} \\ \hline 2 & 5 & 3 \\ \hline 2 & 5 & 4 \\ \hline 4 & 5 & 6 \\ \hline 1 & 2 & 3 \\ \hline \end{array}\end{split}\]

Compute the union of R and S. Which of the following tuples DOES NOT appear in the result?

  1. (2,5,3)
  2. (2,5,4)
  3. (4,5,6)
  4. (1,5,4)

Question 4:¶

Suppose relation R(A,B) has the following tuples:

\[\begin{split}\begin{array}{|c|c|} \hline \textbf{A} & \textbf{B} \\ \hline 1 & 2 \\ \hline 3 & 4 \\ \hline 5 & 6 \\ \hline \end{array}\end{split}\]

and relation S(B,C,D) has de following tuples:

\[\begin{split}\begin{array}{|c|c|c|} \hline \textbf{B} & \textbf{C} & \textbf{D} \\ \hline 2 & 4 & 6 \\ \hline 4 & 6 & 8 \\ \hline 4 & 7 & 9 \\ \hline \end{array}\end{split}\]

Compute the natural-join of R and S. Which of the following tuples is in the result? Assume each tuple has schema (A,B,C,D).

  1. (5,6,4,6)
  2. (1,4,6,8)
  3. (5,6,7,9)
  4. (3,4,7,9)

Question 5:¶

Suppose relation R(A,B,C) has the following tuples:

\[\begin{split}\begin{array}{|c|c|c|} \hline \textbf{A} & \textbf{B} & \textbf{C} \\ \hline 1 & 2 & 3 \\ \hline 4 & 2 & 3 \\ \hline 4 & 5 & 6 \\ \hline 2 & 5 & 3 \\ \hline 1 & 2 & 6 \\ \hline \end{array}\end{split}\]

Compute the projection

\[\pi_{C,B} (R)\]

Which of the following tuples is in the result?

  1. (6,2)
  2. (2,5)
  3. (4,2,3)
